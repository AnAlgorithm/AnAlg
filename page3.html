Rubik's cubes

How to solve a cube

0x0 cube

A 0x0 cube is practically nothing, so you just fiddle in the air as fast as possible, and hit your hand on the stackmat. And you've solved the cube, so it is basically no logic.

1x1 cube

A 1x1 cube also has no logic, the thing is that you need to flick it or turn it, so you can do easily with even one hand. Anyone can solve a 1x1.

2x2 cube

The 2x2 is the smallest cube that needs logic to solve it, but a speedcuber can solve it in about 0.5 to 2 seconds. The world record is 0.49 seconds. But, yet it is easy to solve with a tutorial.

3x3 cube

The 3x3 was the first cube to be built in 1974, by Erno Rubik. The 3x3 can be solved in 2 to 5 minutes, using beginner's method. But an average speedcuber can solve it in 4 to 10 seconds, the world record is 3.47 seconds. It is the 2nd smallest cube to have an algorithm.

Pyraminx

The pyraminx cube is a triangular cube, and it is a cube where it has 3 centres. An easy cube, it's world record is 0.99 seconds. Though a person can't solve it without logic.

Megaminx

The megaminx is in the shape of a dodecahedron, and it has 12 sides. It is a big cube, and beginners method will take you a long time. Instead of a white cross in the 3x3 cube, this has a starting of a star.
